# Emotions and Mind-State Equivalence Principle -by Dhananjay Sharma
This theory suggests that,
	"Emotions are the temporarily altered states of mind brought upon by the effects of temporary chemicals in brain released upon recieving specific stimulus"
Emotions are a subjective experience, but according to the accounts of many people and there experiences, emotions are something that affects not only the outputs of the system but also the way that people arrive at them. For example, say a person is walking down the street and bumps into someone. Normally this said person would just apologize and might give some advice and go away. But, on this day this person was angry, and his reasoning this time might be to blame the other person and ask the other person toapologize. The person is the same but under the influence of an emotion, they not only act differently but are entirely unable to think the way they do normally.
This Observation tells us that emotions are somehow controlling the core architecture of our brain that is involved in making decisions. So, the mechanism actually goes on like this:
- An emotional stimulus triggers a pathway that releases various chemicals in various quantities inside the brain.
- These chemicals change the properties of neurons, effectively changing the modifiers and the delays slightly.
- Since these neurons were controling the entire way our inputs got processed, the fundamental way that our body processes informations gets changed now.
- Our brain retains a temporary memory of the inputs, actions and the way it got to those actions. The brain now consults those memories and decides that this is a different state.
- Our brain then consults Past memories of Accounts of other people experiencing a similar state. The brain finds out that most of them called this state 'X'.
- Our brain then labels this state in its memory as 'X'.
- This state was brought about by the effects of some chemicals that decay eventually, leaving the original architecture back incharge without much alterations.
This temporary new changed way that our brain processes information now is a different mind-state and since our sense of feeling is nothing but the way we process the information and the way various pathways get illuminated, changing the core modifiers essentially changes the way we feel. This new state is our 'Emotion'.
